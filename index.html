Tiffany Monroe

A little bit about me.

My love of storytelling led me, in a roundabout way, to web development. I graduated with a dual degree in English and Creative Writing, then worked as a college writing instructor. I started to code as a hobby. I loved it so much that I decided to go through an immersive program with over 500 hours of instruction in three months. I discovered, then, that writing code is similar to writing poetry. It needs to have the most impact in the fewest lines.

The front end is where code meets creativity. It creates an experience for the user. It tells a story. It’s where my experience teaching digital rhetoric helps me understand how to take an idea and build it into a useful feature through JavaScript, CSS, and some good ol’ HTML. I love learning new frameworks, like Angular or React, and figuring out challenges with my research skills. I also continue to practice building full stack apps.

I take your vision—whether it's Adobe XD, Photoshop, or a drawing on a napkin—and turn it into a pixel perfect match.

I write clean, semantic HTML5 by hand to make sure that the content is always easy to read, maintain, and troubleshoot.

I focus on keeping my CSS lean and fast to load, and I make it a habit to stay up to date on current best practices.

I build responsive, mobile-first apps using vanilla CSS or tools, such as Bootstrap.
